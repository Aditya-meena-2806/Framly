What are Fruits and Vegetables?

Fruits and vegetables are edible parts of plants that are usually consumed as part of a healthy diet. Fruits are sweet or sour and usually juicy, while vegetables are savory and can be eaten raw or cooked.

Why are Fruits and Vegetables Important?

Fruits and vegetables are important because they provide essential vitamins, minerals, and fiber that our bodies need to function properly. Eating a variety of fruits and vegetables can help reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, stroke, and certain types of cancer.

How to Select and Store Fruits and Vegetables?

When selecting fruits and vegetables, choose ones that are in season and ripe for the best flavor and nutritional value. Look for produce that is firm, brightly colored, and free from bruises or blemishes.

When storing fruits and vegetables, keep them in the refrigerator or in a cool, dry place to extend their shelf life. Some fruits and vegetables can also be frozen for later use.
